Student Guide

Navigate Italy step by step

Everything you need to know about living in Siena — from your first documents to your daily routine.

Codice Fiscale (Tax Code)

▾

The codice fiscale is a 16-character alphanumeric code used to identify you in all dealings with Italian public administration. You'll need it for almost everything — opening a bank account, renting an apartment, registering at university.

  • Go to the nearest Agenzia delle Entrate office in Siena (Via Massetana Romana)
  • Bring your passport and a completed Modello AA4/8 form (available at the office)
  • The code is usually issued on the same day, for free
  • You can also apply at the Italian consulate in your home country before arrival
Tip: Get this on your first or second day. Almost every other step depends on having it.

Permesso di Soggiorno (Residence Permit)

▾

If you are a non-EU citizen, you must apply for a permesso di soggiorno within 8 working days of arriving in Italy.

  • Obtain a "Kit Postale" (postal kit) from any post office (Poste Italiane) — it's free
  • Fill in the forms included in the kit carefully
  • Submit the completed kit at the post office (Sportello Amico) — there's a fee of about €30
  • You will receive a receipt, which acts as your temporary permit
  • Attend the appointment at the Questura for fingerprinting
Tip: The university's International Office often runs workshops to help students fill in the forms correctly.

Residency Registration (Comune)

▾

If you plan to stay in Siena for more than 3 months, you can register your residency at the Comune di Siena (City Hall).

  • Go to the Anagrafe office at Palazzo Pubblico, Piazza del Campo
  • Bring your passport, codice fiscale, permesso di soggiorno, and proof of address
  • A municipal officer may visit your address to verify your residence
Tip: Residency registration in Siena can qualify you for reduced bus fares and other local benefits.

Finding Accommodation

▾

Siena has several accommodation options for students. The earlier you start looking, the better.

  • University residences (DSU Toscana): Subsidised housing for eligible students — apply via the DSU Toscana website
  • Private rooms: Check Idealista, Immobiliare.it, and local Facebook groups like "Affitti Siena studenti"
  • Shared apartments: Often the most affordable option
  • Average costs: €300–€550/month for a room, €600–€900 for a studio
Tip: Always visit (or video call) before paying any deposit. Be cautious of listings that seem unusually cheap.

Signing a Rental Contract

▾

Most landlords require a regular rental contract (contratto di locazione), which must be registered with the Agenzia delle Entrate.

  • You will need your codice fiscale to sign any contract
  • Common contract types: 4+4 (standard), 3+2 (transitional), or short-term
  • Expect to pay 1–2 months deposit (cauzione) upfront
  • Always ask for a registered contract — it protects both you and the landlord
Tip: A registered contract is required to register your residency and for the permesso di soggiorno application.

Getting a Bus Pass (Abbonamento)

▾

Siena's urban bus network is operated by Tiemme. Students can get a monthly or annual subscription at a discounted rate.

  • Go to the Tiemme ticket office near Piazza Gramsci
  • Bring your codice fiscale, student ID, and a passport photo
  • Monthly pass for students: approximately €20–€28 for urban lines
  • Single tickets (€1.50) or a pack of 10 are available at tabacchi shops
Tip: If you have registered residency in Siena, you may qualify for the resident rate, which is cheaper.

Getting Around Siena

▾

Siena's historic centre is largely pedestrianised. The main bus terminal is Piazza Gramsci.

  • University campuses are connected by buses — check the Tiemme app for routes
  • Siena is very walkable; most things are within 15–20 minutes on foot
  • For Florence, Flixbus and Sena/Baltour coaches operate from Piazza Gramsci
  • Siena Stazione is about 2km from the centre, connected by bus
Tip: Download the Moovit or Google Maps app — both work well for Siena's bus routes.

Registering with a GP (Medico di Base)

▾

To access Italy's National Health Service (SSN) for free, register with a local general practitioner.

  • Go to the ASL Toscana Sud Est office in Siena
  • Bring your permesso di soggiorno, codice fiscale, and proof of address
  • You'll be assigned a GP in your area, or you can choose one from a list
  • EU citizens can use the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) directly
Tip: Registration is free and gives you access to GP visits, prescriptions, and referrals at no cost.

Emergencies & Pharmacy

▾

Italy has an excellent network of pharmacies and hospitals. For emergencies, you always have access to free treatment.

  • Emergency number: 118 (medical), 112 (general emergency)
  • Main hospital: Policlinico Le Scotte, Viale Mario Bracci 16
  • Pharmacies (farmacie) display a green cross
  • Night pharmacies (farmacie di turno) rotate — check the list posted on any pharmacy window
Tip: The university also has a medical centre (ambulatorio) for students.

Opening a Bank Account

▾

Having an Italian bank account makes life much easier — for paying rent, utilities, and receiving scholarship payments.

  • You'll need: passport, codice fiscale, permesso di soggiorno, and proof of address
  • Main banks in Siena: Monte dei Paschi di Siena (MPS), UniCredit, Intesa Sanpaolo
  • Alternatively, consider digital accounts like N26, Revolut, or Wise
  • Traditional accounts often have monthly fees (€5–€10); student accounts may be free
Tip: Many students use Revolut/N26 for day-to-day spending while waiting for their permesso di soggiorno to open a traditional account.

Enrolling & Student ID

▾

Whether you're at Università degli Studi di Siena or Università per Stranieri di Siena, enrolment involves a few key steps.

  • Complete online pre-enrolment through the university's student portal (ESSE3)
  • Bring original documents to the segreteria studenti: passport, qualification certificates, and codice fiscale
  • Pay the university fees (tasse universitarie); DSU borse di studio can reduce or waive them
  • Collect your student card — needed for library access, discounts, and bus passes
Tip: Apply for the DSU scholarship early — it can cover tuition, canteen meals, and housing.
2
🍽️

University Canteen & Services

▾

DSU Toscana manages university canteens (mense) in Siena. With the right registration, meals can be heavily subsidised.

  • Register on the DSU Toscana portal to access subsidised meals (as low as €1–€2.60)
  • Main canteens: Mensa di San Miniato, Mensa di Via Bandini
  • Show your badge or use the DSU app at the entrance
  • Library access is free with your student card
Tip: Even without the DSU scholarship, you can use the canteen at the standard student rate (~€5–€6 for a full meal).

About Us

Born from experience

Siena Connect was created by three international students who lived through the challenges of arriving in a new country and wanted to make it easier for those who come after them.

Our Story

We were those students

When we arrived in Siena, we each faced the same overwhelming experience: a new country, a new language, and a pile of bureaucratic tasks with no clear guide. We searched across dozens of websites, asked older students, made mistakes, and slowly found our way.

After going through this ourselves, we asked: why should every new student start from scratch? The information exists. The community is there. It just needs a place to come together.

Siena Connect is our answer — built with the support of the university, local institutions, and student organisations.
