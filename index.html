Common questions

Quick answers

Is Siena Connect free? ▾
Yes, completely free for all international students. We never charge students for information, the guide, or community access. Our costs are covered by local business partners who want to reach you.
Who built this? ▾
Siena Connect was founded by Satish Chand Gupta, an international student from India studying at the University of Siena. He built it after going through the same experience himself. A small team of fellow students helps maintain and grow it.
I'm an EU student — is this relevant for me? ▾
Yes. While some sections focus on non-EU requirements like the permesso di soggiorno, the guide covers housing, transport, health, banking, and university services which are relevant for all international students regardless of nationality.
How accurate is the information? ▾
We update the guide regularly and cross-check with official sources. That said, Italian bureaucracy changes. Always verify important details with the relevant office before acting. If you spot something outdated, let us know through the community.
Can I contribute or become a mentor? ▾
Yes. If you have been in Siena for at least 6 months and want to help newly arriving students, contact us through the About page. We are building a verified mentor network.

Codice Fiscale (Tax Code)

▾

A 16-character code used in all dealings with Italian public administration. Needed for a bank account, rental contract, university registration, and almost everything else.

  • Go to Agenzia delle Entrate, Via Massetana Romana — bring your passport
  • Fill in Modello AA4/8 (available at the office)
  • Issued on the spot, for free
  • Can also be obtained at the Italian consulate before you arrive
Tip: Get this on your first or second day. Almost everything else depends on it.
📋

Permesso di Soggiorno

▾

Non-EU students must apply within 8 working days of arriving in Italy.

  • Get a Kit Postale from any Poste Italiane branch — it's free
  • Fill in the forms carefully (the International Office runs workshops)
  • Submit at the post office Sportello Amico — fee around €30
  • Keep the receipt — it's your temporary permit
  • Attend fingerprinting appointment at the Questura when called
Tip: Timeline is typically 6–10 weeks. This is normal. Do not panic.
🏛️

Residency Registration

▾

If staying more than 3 months, you can register residency at the Comune di Siena.

  • Anagrafe office at Palazzo Pubblico, Piazza del Campo
  • Bring passport, codice fiscale, permesso di soggiorno, proof of address
  • A municipal officer may visit to verify your address
Tip: Residency unlocks reduced bus fares and other local benefits.
🔍

Finding Accommodation

▾

Start looking as early as possible — Siena fills up fast before semester starts.

  • DSU Toscana residences: subsidised, apply early at dsu.toscana.it
  • Private rooms: Idealista, Immobiliare.it, local Facebook groups
  • Average: €300–550/month for a room, €600–900 for a studio
  • Always verify the landlord's identity and inspect before paying any deposit
Tip: Check the Siena Connect Housing Board — rooms posted by students leaving Siena, no agency fees.
📝

Signing a Rental Contract

▾

Any legitimate rental contract must be registered with the Agenzia delle Entrate.

  • You need your codice fiscale to sign any contract
  • Expect 1–2 months deposit (cauzione) upfront
  • Always request a registered contract — it protects you legally
  • A registered contract is required for your permesso and residency registration
Tip: Never pay a deposit before seeing a signed, registered contract.
🎫

Getting a Bus Pass

▾

Siena's buses are operated by Tiemme. Monthly student passes are available at a discount.

  • Tiemme office near Piazza Gramsci — bring codice fiscale, student ID, and a photo
  • Monthly student pass: approximately €20–28 for urban lines
  • Single tickets (€1.50) at any tabacchi shop
Tip: With registered residency you may qualify for the cheaper resident rate.
🗺️

Getting Around Siena

▾

Siena's historic centre is largely pedestrianised. Most things are within 15–20 minutes on foot.

  • Bus terminal: Piazza Gramsci — main hub for all lines
  • Florence: Flixbus and Baltour coaches from Piazza Gramsci (~1.5 hours)
  • Download Moovit or use Google Maps for real-time routes
👨‍⚕️

Registering with a GP

▾

Register with a local general practitioner to access Italy's National Health Service (SSN) for free.

  • ASL Toscana Sud Est office in Siena
  • Bring permesso di soggiorno, codice fiscale, and proof of address
  • EU students: use your European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) directly
Tip: Free to register. Gives you GP visits, prescriptions, and referrals at no cost.
🏥

Emergencies & Pharmacy

▾
  • Emergency number: 118 (medical) · 112 (general)
  • Main hospital: Policlinico Le Scotte, Viale Mario Bracci 16
  • Pharmacies (farmacie) display a green cross
  • Night pharmacies (di turno) are listed on any pharmacy window
💳

Opening a Bank Account

▾

An Italian or European bank account makes paying rent and receiving scholarships much easier.

  • You need: passport, codice fiscale, permesso, proof of address
  • Traditional banks in Siena: MPS, UniCredit, Intesa Sanpaolo
  • Digital accounts (N26, Revolut, Wise) work without the permesso
  • Student accounts are often fee-free — ask specifically
Tip: Use Revolut or N26 for day-to-day while waiting for your permesso to open a traditional account.
🎓

Enrolling & Student ID

▾
  • Complete online pre-enrolment through ESSE3 (student portal)
  • Bring originals to the segreteria: passport, qualifications, codice fiscale
  • Pay tasse universitarie — apply for DSU scholarship to reduce or waive them
  • Collect your student card — needed for library, canteen, discounts, bus pass
Tip: Apply for the DSU scholarship early — it can cover tuition, meals, and housing.
🍽️

University Canteen & Services

▾
  • Register on DSU Toscana portal for subsidised meals (€1–2.60)
  • Main canteens: Mensa di San Miniato, Mensa di Via Bandini
  • Show badge or DSU app at entrance
  • Library access free with student card

About Us

Born from experience

Siena Connect was founded by Satish Chand Gupta, an international student from India who went through the challenge of settling in Siena alone and decided to build something that makes it easier for every student who comes after.

The Story

Built by a student
who needed it

When Satish arrived in Siena, he faced the same experience most international students face: a new country, a new language, and a pile of bureaucratic tasks with no clear guide. He searched across dozens of websites, asked other students, made mistakes, and slowly found his way.

After going through it himself, he asked: why should every new student start from scratch? The information exists. The community is there. It just needs somewhere to come together.

Siena Connect is that place — built by a student, for students.
