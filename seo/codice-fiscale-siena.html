Codice Fiscale in Siena: Get It on Day 1

Your Italian tax code unlocks everything — a bank account, a SIM card, a lease. Here's exactly how to get it in 30 minutes.

🆓 Free ⏱ 30 minutes 📄 Just your passport ✅ Issued on the spot

What is the Codice Fiscale?

The Codice Fiscale (CF) is Italy's national tax identification code — a 16-character alphanumeric string derived from your name, date of birth, and place of birth. Every person in Italy, resident or not, needs one.

As an international student in Siena, you'll be asked for it when opening a bank account, signing a rental contract, registering with a GP (medico di base), getting a SIM card, or enrolling in your university's systems. Get it on Day 1.

What to Bring

  • Valid passport (mandatory — no substitute accepted)
  • Proof of Italian address — a rental contract, a university letter with your Siena address, or even a written statement works in practice
  • University enrollment letter (helpful but not always required)
Tip: If you don't have a confirmed address yet, bring your university acceptance letter with the campus address. Staff at the Siena office are accustomed to new arrivals and will usually accept it.

Step-by-Step

  1. 1
    Arrive early The queue fills up quickly, especially in September. Aim to arrive 15 minutes before opening.
  2. 2
    Take a queue number At the entrance, take a ticket from the machine. Select the option for "Codice Fiscale" or ask staff.
  3. 3
    Wait to be called Wait times vary from 10–40 minutes depending on the day. Bring something to read.
  4. 4
    Present your passport The clerk will fill out the form for you. You don't need to prepare any paperwork in advance.
  5. 5
    Receive your certificate on the spot You'll get a printed certificate immediately. A plastic card arrives by post in 2–3 weeks. Both are legally valid.

Can I Apply Online?

Italian residents can apply online via the Agenzia delle Entrate website if they already have a SPID or CIE. As a new arrival, you won't have either yet — go in person on Day 1. It's fast and free.

What Can I Do with It?

  • Open an Italian bank account (Intesa Sanpaolo, Banca MPS, N26, etc.)
  • Get an Italian SIM card (TIM, Vodafone, WindTre — all ask for it)
  • Sign your rental contract
  • Register with the local health service (ASL Siena) for a free GP
  • Access university systems and student discounts
  • Apply for a residence permit (Permesso di Soggiorno)
Your CF code is permanent and unique to you — it doesn't change if you move cities or change your name after marriage (though a name change may require an update).

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it free?

Yes. Completely free. No fee, no stamp duty, nothing to pay.

Do I need an appointment?

No appointment needed for a Codice Fiscale at the Siena office — just walk in and take a queue number.

Can I use it the same day?

Yes. The paper certificate is valid immediately. Show it to a SIM store or bank the same afternoon.

What if I lose it?

You can get a duplicate at any Agenzia delle Entrate office or generate a copy online once you have SPID. Your CF code never changes, so any replacement will show the same number.

Can I calculate my CF in advance?

Yes — there are online calculators (search "codice fiscale calculator"). The code is deterministic, so it's the same whether you calculate it or get it officially. However, only the official certificate from Agenzia delle Entrate is accepted by banks and landlords.
