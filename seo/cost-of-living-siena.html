Transport Costs

Siena's historic centre is completely walkable — most university buildings are within 20 minutes on foot from the main residential areas. A monthly Tiemme bus pass (€25–€35 with student discount) covers the city network and nearby towns.

If you live in the suburbs (Taverne, Isola d'Arbia), a second-hand scooter or bike gives you independence. Budget around €400–€800 for a used 50cc scooter + insurance.

Florence is 1.5 hours by train (Siena–Empoli–Florence, €9–€14) and well worth the day trip on weekends.

Healthcare Costs

Once you have your Codice Fiscale and Permesso di Soggiorno, you can register with the Italian National Health Service (SSN) at ASL Siena. Registration is free and gives you access to:

  • A GP (medico di base) — free consultations
  • Prescriptions at heavily subsidised prices (€2–€5 co-pay)
  • Specialist referrals at subsidised rates
  • Emergency care — free at any hospital

If you're an EU citizen with an EHIC card, you're already covered for emergency and essential care before you register with SSN. Non-EU students should register with SSN as soon as their Permesso is approved.

Student Discounts Worth Knowing

  • University canteen (mensa) meals from €2.60 with your student card — best value in the city
  • Tiemme bus — student monthly pass is ~30% cheaper than standard
  • Musei Nazionali (state museums) — free for EU students under 25; reduced for others
  • CUS Siena gym + sports facilities — available to all enrolled students from €15/month
  • Many bars offer free aperitivo buffet with a drink (€5–€7) from 18:00–20:00
  • Lidl and Eurospin are the cheapest supermarkets; Conad has the best fresh produce
  • Rail discounts: Carta Giovani (free if under 26 via app IO) gives 25% off Trenitalia regional trains
Borsa di Studio (Merit Scholarship): ARDSU Toscana offers merit + means-tested scholarships covering tuition, a university room, and a cash grant of ~€3,000–€5,000/year. If you qualify, this changes your budget picture completely. Check the ARDSU website each April when applications open.

Siena vs Other Italian Student Cities

Siena is notably cheaper than Florence, Milan, or Rome for housing — often 30–40% less for equivalent accommodation. Day-to-day costs (food, coffee, transport) are comparable. The trade-off is a smaller city with fewer job opportunities, but for pure study + quality of life, Siena punches well above its weight.

One-Off Arrival Costs

  • Permesso di Soggiorno kit: ~€70
  • Rental deposit: 1–2 months rent (€350–€900)
  • Bedding, kitchenware, basic furniture: €100–€250 (IKEA Firenze, or local second-hand)
  • SIM card setup: €10–€20
  • Passport photos for admin: €5–€10
  • Initial grocery stock: €50–€80

Total one-off arrival budget: expect to have €800–€1,500 available beyond your first month's rent before you arrive.
