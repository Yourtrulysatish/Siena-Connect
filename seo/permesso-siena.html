Permesso di Soggiorno in Siena: The Complete Student Guide

Non-EU students must apply for a residence permit within 8 days of arrival. Here's every step — documents, post office kit, Questura appointment, and timelines.

🇪🇺 Non-EU students 📅 8 days to apply 💶 ~€70 fee ⏳ 3–6 months processing

Who Needs a Permesso di Soggiorno?

If you are a non-EU citizen coming to study in Italy, you must apply for a Permesso di Soggiorno per Motivi di Studio (residence permit for study purposes) within 8 calendar days of entering Italy. EU/EEA/Swiss citizens do not need one — you only need to register your residence at the Comune di Siena.

⚠ The 8-day deadline is strict. Missing it can complicate your application and may affect future visa renewals. Start on Day 1 or 2.

Documents You'll Need

🛂
Valid passport All pages with stamps. Bring photocopies of every page.
✈️
Entry visa (student) The type-D national visa issued by the Italian consulate in your country.
🎓
University enrollment letter Official letter from your university confirming enrollment for the current academic year.
🏠
Proof of address Rental contract or university accommodation letter with your Siena address.
💰
Proof of financial means Bank statement showing ≥ €5,836/year (2026 threshold), scholarship letter, or family income declaration.
📋
Codice Fiscale Your Italian tax code. Get this first — it's needed on the application form.
📸
Passport photos 4 × passport-sized photos (35×45mm, white background).
🏥
Health insurance EHIC / travel insurance or proof you've enrolled in Italy's SSN (national health service).

Step-by-Step Application

  1. 1
    Get your Codice Fiscale first You need it on the application form. Visit the Agenzia delle Entrate on Day 1 — it takes 30 minutes and is free. Full guide →
  2. 2
    Buy the "kit busta" at a post office Go to any Poste Italiane with Sportello Amico service. Ask for the kit per permesso di soggiorno (the yellow envelope kit). It costs approximately €70.46, which covers the administrative fee and postage. The kit contains: Modello 1 (application form), the yellow envelope, and a receipt booklet.
  3. 3
    Fill in Modello 1 Complete the form in block capitals. Key fields: personal data, Codice Fiscale, reason for stay (Motivi di Studio), entry date, address in Siena, passport details. University staff or the international office can help if you get stuck.
  4. 4
    Assemble your envelope Insert into the yellow envelope: the completed Modello 1, photocopies of passport (all pages), photocopy of your student visa, enrollment letter, proof of address, financial means proof, and 2 passport photos.
  5. 5
    Submit at the Sportello Amico post office Hand over the sealed envelope at the counter. The clerk scans it and gives you a receipt with a future appointment date at the Questura (police headquarters). Keep this receipt safely — it's your proof of legal stay while the application is processed.
  6. 6
    Attend your Questura appointment The appointment is at Questura di Siena. Bring your originals (passport, visa, enrollment letter) plus everything you put in the envelope. You'll be fingerprinted and photographed. The whole appointment takes about 20–40 minutes.
  7. 7
    Wait for the SMS Processing takes 3–6 months on average. You'll receive an SMS when your permit is ready. In the meantime, your post office receipt + passport is your legal proof of stay.
  8. 8
    Collect your Permesso Return to the Questura on the SMS notification date with your receipt and passport. Sign for your permit card.

Renewal

Student permits are valid for the duration of your academic year (typically 1 year). You must renew at least 60 days before expiry. The renewal process is the same: new kit from the post office, same documents plus your existing permit, same Questura appointment flow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I travel within the Schengen area while my application is pending?

Yes, with your receipt (ricevuta) from the post office plus your passport and valid visa. This combination acts as a temporary travel document within Schengen.

What if I miss the 8-day deadline?

Apply as soon as possible regardless. Document any unavoidable reason for the delay (illness, bank holiday, etc.). The Questura has some discretion for reasonable delays, but don't test it — aim for Day 1–3.

Does my university help with this?

Most Siena universities (Università di Siena, UNISTRASI, Siena School for Liberal Arts) have an international student office that holds group sessions at the beginning of each semester. Check with your enrollment coordinator.

EU students — do you need to do anything?

No permit needed. EU/EEA/Swiss citizens staying more than 3 months should register their residence (residenza) at the Comune di Siena — Ufficio Anagrafe, Via Sallustio Bandini 34. Bring your passport and proof of address.
