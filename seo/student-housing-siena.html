Siena Housing Market — Quick Overview

Siena is a compact city of about 54,000 people with a significant student population (~20,000). The historic centre is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which means rents near Piazza del Campo are higher and supply is limited. Most students live in the contrade just outside the walls or in suburban areas like Taverne d'Arbia and Isola d'Arbia, connected by bus.

Siena's housing market moves fast in July–September as students arrive for the academic year. If you're arriving in September, start searching in June.

Best Neighbourhoods for Students

Centro Storico
€400–€550/mo single
Walk to everything. Noisy during Palio season. Limited supply, premium price. Best for short stays or if budget isn't a constraint.
Fontebranda / Castelvecchio
€350–€480/mo single
Just below the walls. Quieter, still walkable to campus. Good mix of long-term rentals and student flats.
San Miniato / Viale Mazzini
€320–€430/mo single
South of the centre near Policlinico. Popular with medical and science students. Regular bus connections.
Taverne d'Arbia
€280–€360/mo single
10km south, bus to centre in ~20 min. Cheapest option. Good for students on tight budgets who don't mind commuting.
Isola d'Arbia
€270–€350/mo single
Quiet village, 12km from centre. Very affordable but bus-dependent. Better with a scooter or bike.
Due Ponti / Petriccio
€300–€400/mo single
North-east of the walls. Mixed residential area. Newer buildings, more modern flats than the historic centre.

Red Flags to Avoid

⚠ No written contract — Never pay rent without a signed contract (contratto di locazione) registered with the Agenzia delle Entrate. An unregistered contract offers you no legal protection.
  • Landlord asks for rent in cash only, refuses to give receipts
  • Deposit more than 3 months' rent (illegal in Italy)
  • Listing photos don't match the flat when you visit — ask for a video call before travelling
  • Landlord reluctant to put your name on the lease as a foreign national
  • Requests a "reservation fee" before you've seen any paperwork
  • No utility meters or meters shared with other flats (unclear bills)

Signing a Rental Contract as an International Student

Most Italian landlords rent on a Contratto Transitorio (transitional contract, 1–18 months) or Contratto 4+4 (4-year renewable). Students are usually offered the transitional contract.

You'll need: Codice Fiscale, a copy of your passport, proof of income / scholarship / parental guarantor, and sometimes a reference from your university. If the landlord is nervous about renting to a foreign national, offer 2 months deposit upfront or ask your university to provide a guarantee letter.

Tip: The contract must be registered (registrato) with the Agenzia delle Entrate within 30 days of signing. This is the landlord's responsibility, but ask for the registration receipt (ricevuta di registrazione) — you'll need it for your Permesso di Soggiorno application.

University Accommodation (ARDSU)

ARDSU Siena manages student residences across the city, including Residenza Santa Chiara and Residenza Il Campo. Prices range from €150 to €350/month including utilities — significantly below market rate.

Places are allocated based on the Borsa di Studio (merit + means test). Apply each spring on the ARDSU Toscana portal. Even if you don't qualify for the scholarship, you may still apply for a room at a reduced rate.
